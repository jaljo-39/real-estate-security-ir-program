## Risk Assessment Methodology

### 1. Asset Identification
Critical assets identified include:
- Email systems
- Wire transfer communication channels
- Client PII and financial records

### 2. Threat Identification
Common threats considered:
- Phishing & Vishing attacks
- Business Email Compromise 
- Insider misuse
- Wire fraud

### 3. Vulnerability Identification
The organization identified the following key vulnerabilities:
- Lack of multi-factor authentication (MFA) for email access
- Excessive user permissions on shared drives
- Limited security awareness training
- Inconsistent vendor security review process

### 4. Risk Scoring Model
Risk is calculated using the following formula:
Risk = Likelihood x Impact

Likelihood Scale
Rating	Description
1 – Low	Unlikely to occur
2 – Medium	Possible occurrence
3 – High	Likely to occur
Impact Scale
Rating	Description
1 – Low	Minimal operational disruption
2 – Medium	Financial or reputational damage
3 – High	Significant financial loss, legal exposure, or regulatory impact

###5. Risk Treatment Strategy

Identified risks will be addressed using one of the following strategies:

Mitigate – Implement controls to reduce likelihood or impact

Accept – Acknowledge and monitor the risk

Transfer – Shift risk through insurance or contractual agreements

Avoid – Eliminate the activity creating the risk
